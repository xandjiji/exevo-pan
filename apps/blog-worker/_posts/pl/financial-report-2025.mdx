2025 was a pretty packed year for Cipsoft. Here is a summary of the most relevant events that could explain this strong result:
- <strong>Monk announcement:</strong> they really stole the timeline with this one. There was a ton of media coverage of this and you can bet the game received a lot of new and old players back because of this. <small>(Februrary/2025)</small>
- <strong>MultiPayer:</strong> a payment system for other game business. <small>(March/2025)</small>
- <strong>The Settlers Online:</strong> absorbed the development and operations of a game from Ubisoft Blue Byte <small>(April/2025)</small>
- <strong>Great Winter and Summer updates:</strong> a lot of quality of life upgrades (Spell opacity, multi-action hotkeys, imbuement scrolls, content for newcomers) and well received content (weapon proficiency and weekly tasks)

Good stuff considering that they allocated a lot of resources for the fifth vocation.


All this effort indeed seem to have moved the needle for Cipsoft. We can't exactly tell how much of the revenue increase can be attributed to each event, but I would guess the <strong>Monk release</strong> had the biggest impact 🚀

## Most popular store cosmetics

Since our previous analysis, it seems that the list for most popular store mounts and outfits haven't changed much. Around `40%` of all store outfits and `52%` of all store mounts were found in less than `1%` of the auctions. This _could_ indicate that Cipsoft's research for new themes might be lacking.

Also, it seems that store cosmetics that that had some sort of special deal performed much better. Perhaps Cipsoft could experiment a little bit more with special deals or limited content in order to increase their revenue 📈
